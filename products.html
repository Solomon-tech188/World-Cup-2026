Aluminum angled connectors are versatile hardware pieces used to securely join aluminum profiles or tubes at specific angles without requiring specialized machining like drilling or threading. They are widely used in industrial frameworks, modular automation, and custom furniture for their lightweight, rust-resistant, and high-load-bearing properties.

L Connector

Aluminum T-connectors (also called T-slot joint plates or T-brackets) join aluminum profiles or tubes perpendicular to each other, forming a "T" shape. They are essential for extending frames, adding internal support crossbars, or linking multiple structural sections together.

T Connector

Aluminum slide connectors (also commonly referred to as straight-line connectors, linear bar connectors, or slide-in joint plates) are specialized hardware components designed to slide directly into the T-slots or internal channels of aluminum profiles. They are primarily used to extend frame lengths end-to-end or facilitate smooth, adjustable linear movement along a track.

A window handle is the primary mechanism used to latch, unlatch, lock, and operate a window. Beyond opening and closing glass panes, specific handle designs serve as critical components for home security, child safety, smart home integration, and building ventilation.

door lock for aluminum doors is a specialized security mechanism engineered to fit inside or onto the narrow-profile frames typical of aluminum entrances. They are primarily used to secure storefronts, commercial offices, residential patios, and heavy-traffic entry points where standard wooden door locks are too wide to install.

Door Lock

An aluminum hinge is a mechanical bearing used to connect two solid objects—such as a door, window, panel, or lid—allowing a limited angle of rotation between them. Known for their high strength-to-weight ratio, non-magnetic properties, and natural resistance to rust, they are heavily used across marine, automotive, industrial, and architectural environments.
